Chess

1/1/2020

My first game of 2020 was a win against a higher rated player, I'll consider that a nice way to start off the year. The game is a 3+2 rated blitz game featuring the Pirc defense, an opening I've begun to play as black as a way to steer the game into a king's indian type position. My opponent allowed me to develop exactly as I wanted and then made the decision to castle on the opposite side, to which I immediately began to attack. The game culminates in a forced mate in three, which I’m happy to have found. After a Stockfish 10 analysis I was pleased to see I played with an average centipawn loss of 32 compared to my opponents 70, and made only 2 mistakes, no inaccuracies or blunders, metrics which I’d say for a blitz game isn’t too bad.

Happy New Years!

12/31/2019

A 5+0 rated blitz game where I have the white pieces, and I open with d4 preparing to play the london system, which my opponent counters with the Englund gambit. This game was cut short by my opponets blunder of Kge7, which trapped their king, allowing me mate in one resulting in a "smoothered mate". Which is when the king is mated by a knight whilst surrounded by his own pieces, cutting off all flight squares.

Happy New Years Eve!
This game is one of my new favorites, and as of 12/29/19, it's my new highest rated victory on lichess. This game was played in the Lichess Winter Marathon tournament, a 24hr long competition of 3+2 blitz chess. I wasn’t planning on competing, rather I was watching International Master Eric Rosen’s Twitch stream of the event, but I ended up joining for fun, and for the chance to play against higher rated and titled players. The tournament had quite the show out, with 13,763 players competing for the virtual trophies given to the top 100, and for other reasons of course. In this game I beat a 2000+ rated player(my blitz rating sits around 1460) with one of my main openings for black, the Scandinavian Defence. My opponent lost to me on time in a almost equal positon.
What a game!
